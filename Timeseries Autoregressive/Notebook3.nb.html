Ljung-Box test:

Null Hypothesis: \(H_0\): at a given lag k, the acf coefficients \(\rho_0= \rho_1= ... =\rho_k=0\). \(H_1\): Coefficients != 0

Note : In statistical hypothesis testing, the p-value or probability value is the probability of obtaining test results at least as extreme as the results actually observed, assuming that the null hypothesis is correct. So here, the P-value of the test is the probability that a chi-square test statistic having the respective degrees of freedom is more extreme than the obtained test statistic.

Interpret results: If the P-value is less than the significance level (0.05), we reject the null hypothesis.
